> A section at the bottom of a page containing copyright, links, and other secondary information.

## Usage

Footers are used to provide consistent navigation and information at the end of every page. They typically contain:

- Copyright notices
- Contact information
- Sitemap links
- Social media icons
- Privacy policy and terms of service links

## Examples

### Basic Footer

```html
<footer class="footer">
  <div class="footer-content">
    <p>&copy; 2024 My Company. All rights reserved.</p>
    <nav>
      <a href="/privacy">Privacy</a>
      <a href="/terms">Terms</a>
    </nav>
  </div>
</footer>
```

### Multi-column Footer

```html
<footer class="footer-grid">
  <div class="column">
    <h4>Product</h4>
    <a href="#">Features</a>
    <a href="#">Pricing</a>
  </div>
  <div class="column">
    <h4>Company</h4>
    <a href="#">About</a>
    <a href="#">Careers</a>
  </div>
  <div class="column">
    <h4>Connect</h4>
    <a href="#">Twitter</a>
    <a href="#">GitHub</a>
  </div>
</footer>
```

## Best Practices

- **Consistency**: Keep the footer consistent across all pages.
- **Hierarchy**: Use clear headings for link groups.
- **Responsiveness**: Ensure the footer stacks vertically on mobile devices.
